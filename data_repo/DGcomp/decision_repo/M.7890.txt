                No 139/2004 and Article 57 of the Agreement on the European Economic Area
1.      On 5 January 2016, the European Commission received notification of a proposed
        concentration pursuant to Article 4 of the Merger Regulation by which the undertakings
        La Compagnie des Cartes Carburant (“LCCC”, France), jointly controlled by the
        undertaking Edenred (France) and private individuals, and UNION TANK Eckstein
        (“UTA”, Germany), jointly controlled by Edenred and Hermes Mineralöl-GmbH
        (“Hermes”, Germany) and Familien-Gesellshaft Eckstein mbH Verwaltungs-KG
        (“Eckstein”, Germany), each acquire within the meaning of Article 3(1)(b) and 3(4) of the
        Merger Regulation joint control of a newly created company constituting a joint venture
        (the “JV”, France) by way of purchase of shares.3
2.      The business activities of the undertakings concerned are:
        -     Edenred provides prepaid corporate services with respect to employee benefits, expense
              management, incentives and rewards and management of public social programs at global level;

   -    LCCC issues and resells fuel and service cards for customers active in commercial goods transport
        and passenger transport in France;
   -    UTA issues fuel and service cards for customers active in commercial goods transport and
        passengers transport in Europe;
   -    Hermes and Eckstein: family-owned holding companies without business operations;
   -    the JV will design, process and operate corporate fuel, toll and parking cards in France
3. After examination of the notification, the European Commission has concluded that the
   notified operation falls within the scope of the Merger Regulation and of paragraph 5(c) of
   the Commission Notice on a simplified procedure for treatment of certain concentrations
   under Council Regulation (EC) No 139/2004.4
4. For the reasons set out in the Notice on a simplified procedure, the European Commission
   has decided not to oppose the notified operation and to declare it compatible with the
   internal market and with the EEA Agreement. This decision is adopted in application of
   Article 6(1)(b) of the Merger Regulation and Article 57 of the EEA Agreement.
