                No 139/2004 and Article 57 of the Agreement on the European Economic Area
1.      On 16 June 2016, the European Commission received notification of a proposed
        concentration pursuant to Article 4 of the Merger Regulation by the undertaking
        Bancopopular- E S.A ("E-Com", Spain), a joint venture controlled by Banco Popular
        Español S.A. ("Banco Popular", Spain) and certain private affiliated funds managed by
        Värde Partners Inc. ("Värde", USA), acquires, within the meaning of Article 3(1)(b) of the
        Merger Regulation, sole control over the payment cards' business (the "Assets") of Banco
        Popular Portugal, S.A. ("BPOP", Portugal), a wholly-owned subsidiary of Banco Popular.3
2.      The business activities of the undertakings concerned are:
             <U+F02D> E-Com provides services related to the issuing of payment cards and, to a lesser
                 extent, to insurance distribution within the Spanish market.

3. After examination of the notification, the European Commission has concluded that the
   notified operation falls within the scope of the Merger Regulation and of paragraph 5(c) of

   under Council Regulation (EC) No 139/2004.4
4. For the reasons set out in the Notice on a simplified procedure, the European Commission
