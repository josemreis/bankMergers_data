1. On 20 August 2001, the Commission received notification of a proposed concentration
     pursuant to Article 4 of Council Regulation (EEC) No. 4064/89 by which the undertakings If
     P&C Holding AB (“If Holding”), which is jointly controlled by the Swedish insurer Skandia
     and the Norwegian company Storebrand ASA (“Storebrand”), the German company Hermes
     Versicherungsbeteiligungen –GmbH (“HVG”), which belongs to the Allianz-group and the
     stated-owned Finish undertaking Finnvera plc (“Finnvera”) acquire within the meaning of
     Article 3(1)(b) of the Council Regulation joint control of the undertaking Finnish Credit
     Insurances Company Ltd (“FCI”) by way of a public bid announced on 11 June 2001.
2. The business activities of the undertakings concerned are :
          -         If Holding: non-life insurance services mainly in Sweden and Norway
          -         HVG: credit insurance

3. After examination of the notification, the Commission has concluded that the notified
   operation falls within the scope of Council Regulation (EEC) No. 4064/89 and of paragraph
   4, subparagraph c of the Commission Notice on a simplified procedure for treatment of
   certain concentrations under Council Regulation (EEC) No 4064/892.
4. For the reasons set out in the Notice on a simplified procedure, the Commission has decided
   not to oppose the notified operation and to declare it compatible with the common market
   and with the EEA Agreement. This decision is adopted in application of Article 6(1)(b) of
   Council Regulation (EEC) No. 4064/89.
