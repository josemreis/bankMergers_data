                Commission decision pursuant to Article 6(1)(b) of Council Regulation (EC)

                No 139/2004 and Article 57 of the Agreement on the European Economic Area
1.      On 20 March 2015, the European Commission received notification of a proposed
        concentration pursuant to Article 4 of the Merger Regulation by which XL Group plc ('XL
        Group', Ireland) acquires within the meaning of Article 3(1)(b) of the Merger Regulation
        control of the whole of Catlin Group Ltd ('Catlin', Bermuda) by way of purchase of
        shares.3
2.      The business activities of the undertakings concerned are:
        <U+F02D>     for XL Group: providing property, casualty and specialty insurance and reinsurance
              products to industrial, commercial and professional firms, insurance companies and other
              enterprises on a worldwide basis.
             <U+F02D> for Catlin: providing specialty property and casualty insurance and reinsurance, as
                 well as life insurance, mostly to professional and business customers worldwide.

3. After examination of the notification, the European Commission has concluded that the
   notified operation falls within the scope of the Merger Regulation and of paragraph 5(c)(i)
   of the Commission Notice on a simplified procedure for treatment of certain concentrations
   under Council Regulation (EC) No 139/2004.4
4. For the reasons set out in the Notice on a simplified procedure, the European Commission
   has decided not to oppose the notified operation and to declare it compatible with the
   internal market and with the EEA Agreement. This decision is adopted in application of
   Article 6(1)(b) of the Merger Regulation and Article 57 of the EEA Agreement.
