Subject: Case No. COMP/M.3574 – Rabobank / BGZ
           Notification of 18/10/2004 pursuant to Article 4 of Council Regulation (EC) No.
           139/20041
           Publication in the Official Journal of the European Communities No. C 263, 26.10.2004,
           page 10.
1.  On 18,10,2004, the Commission received notification of a proposed concentration pursuant
    to Article 4 of Council Regulation (EC) No. 139/2004 by which the undertaking Rabobank
    International Holding BV (“Rabobank”) and the State Treasury of the Republic of Poland
    (“State Treasury”) jointly acquire control over a Polish-based Bank Gospodarki
    Zywnosciowej S.A. (“BGZ”) within the meaning of Article 3(1)(b) of the Council Regulation,
    by way of purchase of shares.
2.  The business activities of the undertakings concerned are:
     BGZ is a Polish commercial bank specialising in the financing of agriculture and food
     economy, regional infrastructure, small and medium enterprises as well as individuals. BGZ
     is active only in Poland, mainly in small towns and villages in the countryside.
     Rabobank is a company belonging to the Rabobank Group. The Rabobank Group is a
     Dutch co-operative bank and credit institution, active in the financial services sector. Its

    The Treasury controls six banks. Apart from BGZ, it directly controls Bank Gospodarstwa
    Krajowego (“BGK”) and Powszechna Kasa Oszczednosci Bank Polski S.A. (“PKO BP”).
    In addition, it indirectly controls the following banks: Bank Ochrony Srodowiska S.A.
    (“BOS”), Bank Inicjatyw Spoleczno-Ekonomicznych S.A. (“BISE”) and Bank Pocztowy
    S.A. (“BP”).
3.  After examination of the notification, the Commission has concluded that the notified
    operation falls within the scope of Council Regulation (EC) No. 139/2004 and of paragraph
    5, subparagraph b, of the Commission Notice on a simplified procedure for treatment of
    certain concentrations under Council Regulation (EC) No 139/20042.
4.  For the reasons set out in the Notice on a simplified procedure, the Commission has decided
    not to oppose the notified operation and to declare it compatible with the common market
    and with the EEA Agreement. This decision is adopted in application of Article 6(1)(b) of
    Council Regulation (EC) No. 139/2004.
